The Complete Guide to Prompt Artisan

Welcome! This tool was created to turn the chaos of random tags into a predictable and manageable creative process. This guide will teach you not only how to use the interface but also how to understand the logic of crafting powerful prompts.

Part 1: Quick Start and Interface

How to Enter Tags (The Main Rule)

Separate your tags with a comma. This is the standard and most reliable method, allowing for multi-word tags.

  • Example: 1girl, long hair, blue eyes — these are three separate tags.
  • The application will automatically trim any extra spaces.

Managing Tag Weights

This is a key feature for controlling emphasis. To change a weight:

  1. Place your cursor right after the desired tag (or select it).
  2. Press the ▲ button to increase the weight (e.g., (tag:1.1)) or ▼ to decrease it ((tag:0.9)).

Pro Tip: Use weights to enhance important details ((masterpiece:1.2)) or weaken problematic ones ((blurry background:0.8)).

Automatic Quality Tags

The "Add quality tags" option is enabled by default. It automatically adds the "gold standard" of tags to the beginning of your prompts for maximum quality (masterpiece, best quality...) and adds boilerplate tags to the negative prompt (worst quality...). It is highly recommended to always keep this enabled.

Preset Management: Import & Export

In this version, your presets are no longer stored in the browser. You manage them using .json files, which ensures reliability and allows you to share your collections.

  1. Saving to Session: When you've created a great prompt, give it a name and click "Save to Session". The preset will appear in the dropdown list and will be available until you close the tab.
  2. Export (Permanent Save): To save all presets from the current session to a file, click "Export (.json)". The browser will prompt you to save a prompt-artisan-presets.json file. This is your personal, permanent collection.
  3. Import: To load presets from a file, click "Import (.json)" and select your previously saved file. All presets from it will appear in the dropdown list.

Pro Tip: Regularly export your collection, especially after creating new successful presets!

Part 2: The Art of Prompting — From Idea to Masterpiece

This section will teach you to think like an artist and a director, filling each block with purpose.

Section 1: Subject and Action

Here, we describe WHO or WHAT is the main focus of the scene and WHAT they are doing.

  • Appearance: Start with the count (1girl, 2boys), then key features.
    • Simple Example: 1girl, smile
    • Complex Example: a beautiful goddess, long flowing silver hair, glowing ethereal eyes
  • Clothing & Accessories: Describe the style, color, and material.
    • Simple Example: red dress
    • Complex Example: wearing intricate futuristic armor, glowing blue circuits, holding a plasma sword
  • Pose & Action: Verbs are your best friends. Make the scene feel alive.
    • Static Poses: standing, sitting, looking at viewer
    • Dynamic Actions: running through a street, jumping between rooftops, casting a spell

Section 2: Composition, Scene, and Style

Here, we create the environment and mood, turning a simple picture into a story.

  • Environment (Location): Where is the action taking place? Be specific.
    • Simple Example: in a forest
    • Complex Example: on the bridge of a massive starship, overlooking a swirling nebula through the main viewport
    • Pro Tip: Add time of day and weather: cyberpunk city street at night, heavy rain, neon reflections on wet pavement.
  • Lighting & Atmosphere: Light is 90% of the mood.
    • Keywords: cinematic lighting, volumetric lighting, rim lighting, dramatic lighting.
    • Atmosphere: moody, ethereal, grim dark, cheerful, epic.
    • Complex Example: god rays shining through the canopy of an ancient forest, morning mist, ethereal atmosphere
  • Camera & Composition: You are the director! Control the camera.

    Common Shots and Angles:

    • Shot Type: close-up shot, medium shot, full body shot, cowboy shot.
    • Angle: from above, from below, dutch angle, eye-level shot.
    • Lens Effects: depth of field, bokeh, blurry background, lens flare.
    • Complex Example: dynamic low-angle shot, from behind, capturing the hero looking up at a giant monster, cinematic depth of field
  • Art Style & Quality: What style will your work be in?
    • Photography: photorealistic, photograph, 8k uhd
    • Painting: oil painting, watercolor, concept art, matte painting
    • Anime: anime style, key visual, studio ghibli style
    • Illustration: comic book style, flat illustration, vector art

Part 3: Professional Techniques

Tag Order Matters!

The neural network pays the most attention to the first tags. A professional prompt is structured in this order:

  1. Quality: masterpiece, best quality... (The app does this for you!)
  2. Style: concept art, by greg rutkowski...
  3. Main Subject & Key Features: 1girl, long hair...
  4. Environment & Secondary Details: in a forest...
  5. Composition & Light: cinematic lighting, from below...

Deconstructing a Complex Prompt

Goal: "An epic concept art of a female warrior in the rain in a cyberpunk city, shot from below."

Assembling in Artisan:

  • Style: concept art, photorealistic
  • Appearance: 1girl, strong female warrior, determined expression
  • Clothing: wearing futuristic tactical suit, glowing neon details
  • Action: standing in the middle of a street
  • Location: cyberpunk city at night, heavy rain
  • Atmosphere: cinematic lighting, neon reflections on wet pavement, dramatic atmosphere
  • Camera: (full body shot:1.1), (from below:1.2)

Experiment! You now have all the knowledge needed to create true masterpieces.
